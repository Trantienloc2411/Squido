About the Book

Starting off in an eighteenth-century London, this book invites readers to an exciting journey. The lifelong fight of main protagonists come solving. The life of the fight against the biggest villains, criminals and cons of his era.

In the heart of Georgian London, young Thomas Hawkins finds himself thrust into a world of intrigue and danger when he discovers a mysterious manuscript that could topple the monarchy. As he navigates the treacherous streets of the city, he must forge unlikely alliances and outwit cunning adversaries who will stop at nothing to possess the manuscript's secrets.

With its richly detailed historical setting, complex characters, and fast-paced plot, "Abstract Design" offers readers an immersive experience into a pivotal moment in history while exploring timeless themes of loyalty, courage, and the pursuit of justice.

Key Features
  • Meticulously researched historical setting
  • Complex, multi-dimensional characters
  • Intricate plot with unexpected twists
  • Exploration of social and political issues of the era
  • Blend of historical fact and compelling fiction

John Smith

John Smith is an award-winning author of historical fiction and thrillers. Born in Edinburgh, Scotland, he developed a passion for history and storytelling at an early age. After studying History at Oxford University, he worked as a museum curator before turning to writing full-time.

His debut novel, "The Clockmaker's Daughter," won the Historical Fiction Award in 2018, and his books have been translated into 28 languages. Known for his meticulous research and vivid prose, Smith brings historical periods to life with remarkable authenticity and creates characters that resonate with readers long after they've finished his books.

When not writing, John enjoys traveling to historical sites, collecting antique books, and giving lectures on history and the craft of writing. He currently lives in London with his wife and two children.
